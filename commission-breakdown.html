Frequently Asked Questions

How are bonuses taxed?
Bonuses are ordinary income, so federal and state withholding often applies at a flat supplemental rate (commonly 22% federally) unless your employer uses your W-4 to aggregate pay. FICA also applies up to applicable wage caps. Your actual tax bill depends on your total year income, credits, and deductions—use this calculator for allocation planning, then confirm withholding with payroll or a tax pro.
How should I split my bonus paycheck?
Start with must-pay items (taxes you will owe beyond withholding, emergency fund floor, high-interest debt minimums), then assign intentional percentages to savings, investments, and lifestyle. Many people prioritize wiping out double-digit APR debt before funding taxable investing. This tool lets you model percentages until they total 100% so you can commit before the money hits checking.
Should I put my bonus in savings or investments?
If you lack three to six months of core expenses in cash, building or topping off a high-yield savings account usually comes first. Once that base exists, long-term goals (401(k), IRA, brokerage) can take a larger slice, especially if your interest costs are low. There is no single right split—use the percentages here to stress-test how aggressive each choice feels.
Can I use this calculator for commission checks?
Yes. Enter the net or gross commission amount you want to plan from, then treat each row as a bucket the same way you would for a bonus. If commissions vary month to month, run a new scenario each time you are paid so your allocation matches the actual check size.
